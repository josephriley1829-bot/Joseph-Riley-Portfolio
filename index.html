Selected Work

Four projects organized like a studio walk-through.

Scroll through each project as a complete visual sequence. Large images create impact; smaller moments let the drawings breathe.

01

Vertex 6

A bar, restaurant, and art gallery on East 6th Street in Austin. The design uses slanted structural frames, layered windows, and a vertical circulation core to turn the building into a bold urban marker.

02

Art Canyon Villa

A desert villa carved into a canyon condition. The project contrasts heavy terrain with soft roof movement, wood texture, open terraces, and framed moments of shade.

03

Art School

A study in education, gathering, and procession. The project combines model making, sectional thinking, public seating, and circulation diagrams to communicate how people move through a creative campus.

04

The B.E.A.N.

A civic-scaled think tank environment shaped by sweeping concrete lines, transparent public edges, rooftop gardens, and open interior gathering zones.

Profile

Senior architecture student at Texas Tech University

I am interested in architecture that feels alive: spaces with depth, hierarchy, beauty, and a clear relationship between people and place. My work uses renderings, physical models, drawings, and digital tools to communicate atmosphere and design intent.
